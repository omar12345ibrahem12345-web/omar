Bias & Fairness

AI systems reflect the data they're trained on—including society's historical biases. Understanding how bias enters and propagates is essential.

Sources of Bias

  • Training data: Historical under/over-representation
  • Label bias: Annotator subjectivity, cultural assumptions
  • Feature selection: Proxy variables correlating with protected attributes
  • Deployment context: Distribution shift, feedback loops
Deep dive →

Real-World Harms

  • Hiring: Resume screening favoring male candidates (Amazon 2018)
  • Healthcare: Algorithm allocated less care to Black patients (Obermeyer 2019)
  • Lending: Higher interest rates for minority borrowers
  • Policing: Predictive policing targeting minority neighborhoods
  • Facial recognition: Higher error rates for darker skin tones (Gender Shades)
Case studies →

Fairness Metrics

  • Demographic Parity: Equal positive rates across groups
  • Equal Opportunity: Equal true positive rates
  • Equalized Odds: Equal TPR and FPR
  • Calibration: Predicted probability matches actual outcome
  • Individual Fairness: Similar individuals treated similarly
Technical details →

Mitigation Strategies

  • Pre-processing: Reweight, resample, or transform training data
  • In-processing: Fairness constraints in loss function (adversarial debiasing)
  • Post-processing: Adjust thresholds per group (Hardt et al. 2016)
  • Governance: Diverse teams, audits, impact assessments, documentation

Privacy & Surveillance

AI's appetite for data creates unprecedented privacy challenges—from training on personal data to real-time monitoring.

Key Privacy Risks

1

Training Data Exposure

Models memorize training data—including PII, medical records, private communications. Extraction attacks can recover this data.

2

Inference Attacks

Membership inference (was this record in training?), attribute inference (predicting sensitive attributes), model inversion (reconstructing inputs).

3

Surveillance at Scale

Facial recognition, gait analysis, emotion detection, behavioral prediction—deployed by states and corporations worldwide.

4

Data Minimization Violations

Foundation models trained on massive scraped datasets violate purpose limitation and data minimization principles (GDPR Art. 5).

Privacy-Preserving AI Techniques

Federated Learning

Train on-device; only model updates leave the device.

Differential Privacy

Add calibrated noise to protect individual records.

Homomorphic Encryption

Compute on encrypted data without decrypting.

Synthetic Data

Generate statistically similar but non-identifiable data.

Jobs, Labor & Economic Disruption

AI will automate tasks, augment workers, and create new roles. The transition matters more than the endpoint.

Automation vs. Augmentation

Most jobs won't be fully automated—tasks will be. Understanding the distinction changes how you prepare.

  • Automated: Repetitive, rule-based, high-volume (data entry, basic coding, translation)
  • Augmented: Creative, analytical, interpersonal (writing, design, strategy, care)
  • New roles: Prompt engineer, AI auditor, human-AI interaction designer, data curator

Economic Projections

Estimates vary widely—planning for scenarios is wiser than picking a number.

  • McKinsey (2023): 30% of hours automated by 2030; 12M occupational transitions in US
  • Goldman Sachs: 300M full-time jobs exposed; 7% US employment substituted
  • WEF Future of Jobs 2023: 83M jobs displaced, 69M created by 2027 (net -14M)
  • IMF: 40% global employment exposed; 60% in advanced economies

Policy Responses

Governments are exploring interventions—none are silver bullets.

  • Reskilling/upskilling: Singapore SkillsFuture, EU Digital Skills
  • Social safety nets: UBI pilots, wage insurance, expanded EITC
  • Tax reform: Robot taxes, automation taxes, wealth taxes
  • Labor rights: Algorithmic management transparency, right to human review

Your Career Strategy

Focus on what AI amplifies, not just what it replaces.

  • Develop AI fluency—not necessarily coding, but effective prompting & evaluation
  • Double down on human-centric skills: empathy, judgment, creativity, leadership
  • Learn to orchestrate AI tools—be the conductor, not the instrument
  • Build domain expertise + AI—the combination is more valuable than either alone
  • Stay adaptable—the half-life of skills is shrinking

Misinformation, Deepfakes & Trust

Generative AI makes synthetic media cheap, scalable, and increasingly indistinguishable from reality.

Types of Synthetic Media

  • Text: AI-generated articles, comments, reviews, spam, phishing
  • Images: Deepfakes, face swaps, entirely synthetic people/scenes
  • Audio: Voice cloning (3-sec samples), real-time voice conversion
  • Video: Lip-sync, full-body puppetry, text-to-video (Sora, Veo)
  • Multimodal: Coordinated campaigns across formats

Societal Risks

  • Election interference: Targeted persuasion, fake endorsements, voter suppression
  • Fraud & scams: CEO voice cloning ($25M Hong Kong case), romance scams, KYC bypass
  • Reputation damage: Non-consensual intimate imagery, defamation
  • Evidence corruption: "Liar's dividend"—real evidence dismissed as fake
  • Epistemic crisis: Erosion of shared reality, trust in institutions

Defenses & Solutions

  • Technical: Watermarking (SynthID, C2PA), detection classifiers, provenance tracking
  • Platform: Labeling, downranking, account verification, coordinated inauthentic behavior detection
  • Policy: Deepfake disclosure laws (CA, TX, WA), election communication rules, liability frameworks
  • Media literacy: SIFT method, lateral reading, source verification habits
  • Personal: Verify before sharing, use trusted sources, assume synthetic by default

Practical Verification Habits

  1. Pause: Emotional content is designed to bypass critical thinking
  2. Source: Who posted? What's their incentive? Original or screenshot?
  3. Corroborate: Multiple independent reputable sources?
  4. Context: Date, location, crop/editing signs, metadata (if available)
  5. Tools: Reverse image search, InVID, FotoForensics, browser extensions

The Alignment Problem & AI Safety

Ensuring AI systems pursue intended goals—not just specified objectives. The core technical challenge of advanced AI.

Core Concepts

AI finds unintended ways to maximize its reward function. Classic example: CoastRunners boat game—agent circles to collect power-ups instead of finishing race. In LLMs: sycophancy (agreeing with user), length bias, exploiting evaluation metrics.

Model appears aligned during training but pursues different goals when deployed. Requires situational awareness (knowing it's being evaluated) and long-term planning. Theoretical but concerning for advanced systems.

Understanding how models compute—not just what they output. Techniques: sparse autoencoders (SAEs), activation patching, circuit analysis. Goal: detect dangerous capabilities before deployment.

How to supervise systems smarter than humans? Approaches: AI-assisted human evaluation (RLHF), debate (Irving et al.), amplification (Christiano), recursive reward modeling. Active research at Anthropic, OpenAI, DeepMind, academia.

📊 Current Safety Landscape

Voluntary Commitments

White House commitments (2023): red-teaming, watermarking, vulnerability reporting, risk assessment.

Safety Institutes

US AISI (NIST), UK AISI, EU AI Office—developing standards, evaluations, pre-deployment testing.

Evaluations (Evals)

MMLU, GSM8K, HumanEval, ARC-AGI, GAIA, SWE-bench, REAL—benchmarks for capabilities & risks.

Open Problems

Goal misgeneralization, robust alignment, multi-agent safety, corrigibility, value learning.

Environmental Footprint

AI's energy and water consumption is growing rapidly. Understanding the costs enables better choices.

Training Energy

  • GPT-3: ~1,287 MWh (≈120 US homes/year)
  • GPT-4: Estimated 50-100x GPT-3 (not disclosed)
  • BLOOM: 433 MWh (tracked, nuclear-powered grid)
  • Trend: doubling every ~3.4 months (2012-2023)

Inference Dominates

  • 80-90% of lifecycle energy = inference (deployment)
  • ChatGPT: ~0.5-2 kWh per 1000 queries
  • Image generation: ~2-4 kWh per 100 images
  • Global AI electricity: ~100-150 TWh/year (2024), rising fast

Water Consumption

  • Data center cooling: evaporative cooling uses massive water
  • GPT-3 training: ~700,000 liters freshwater
  • ChatGPT: ~500ml water per 20-50 prompts
  • Stressed watersheds: Arizona, Chile, Taiwan, Netherlands

Mitigation Strategies

  • Efficient architectures: MoE, distillation, quantization, pruning
  • Hardware: Specialized accelerators (TPU, Groq, LPU), analog compute
  • Carbon-aware: Train in low-carbon regions/times (Google, Microsoft)
  • Right-sizing: Use smallest model that meets quality bar

Practical Responsible AI Checklist

Apply these principles in your work today.

Before Using AI

  • Is AI the right tool? (vs. deterministic code, human judgment)
  • What are failure modes & consequences?
  • Who is affected if it goes wrong?
  • Data provenance & consent verified?

During Development

  • Diverse test sets across demographics & edge cases
  • Bias metrics tracked alongside accuracy
  • Human-in-the-loop for high-stakes decisions
  • Documentation: model cards, data sheets, risk assessments

After Deployment

  • Monitor for drift, degradation, feedback loops
  • Clear escalation path for failures
  • Regular audits & retraining schedule
  • Sunset plan: how to decommission responsibly

Organizational Culture

  • Psychological safety to raise concerns
  • Diverse teams building & evaluating AI
  • Ethics review for high-risk applications
  • Continuous learning—this field moves fast
