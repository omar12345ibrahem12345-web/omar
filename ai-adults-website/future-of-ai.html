AGI Timelines: What Experts Say

Predictions span decades. The disagreement reveals more than the consensus.

Soon (2025-2028)

Proponents: Leopold Aschenbrenner, Dario Amodei (Anthropic), Sam Altman (OpenAI), Shane Legg (DeepMind)

  • Scaling laws hold; compute growing 10x/year
  • Algorithmic improvements: 3-5x compute efficiency/year
  • Unhobbling: agents, tool use, long-context, memory
  • "Drop-in remote worker" by 2027 (Aschenbrenner)

Medium (2029-2035)

Proponents: Geoffrey Hinton, Yoshua Bengio, median Metaculus forecasters, many academic researchers

  • Remaining gaps: reasoning, planning, world models
  • Data wall: high-quality text exhausted ~2026
  • Energy/compute constraints; chip supply limits
  • Deployment & safety feedback loops slow progress

Later (2035-2050+)

Proponents: Yann LeCun, Gary Marcus, Rodney Brooks, many cognitive scientists

  • Current paradigm insufficient: no world models, causal reasoning
  • Need new architectures: JEPA, neurosymbolic, active inference
  • Moravec's paradox: sensorimotor > abstract reasoning
  • AGI definition matters: "human-level at all tasks" is very high bar

Never / Ill-Defined

Proponents: Some philosophers, critics of "AGI" as coherent concept

  • Intelligence is multi-dimensional, not a scalar
  • "General" intelligence doesn't exist in nature
  • Moving goalposts: chess, Go, language → each "solved" redefines AGI
  • Better frame: capability profiles, not binary threshold

Emerging Paradigms

Beyond scaling Transformers—the architectural and methodological shifts that could define the next era.

Test-Time Compute Scaling

o1/o3 paradigm: spend more inference compute for better reasoning. Search, verification, self-consistency at test time.

  • Chain-of-thought + tree search (MCTS)
  • Process reward models (PRMs) for step verification
  • Self-consistency / majority voting
  • Trade-off: latency & cost for accuracy

Agentic Workflows

LLMs as orchestrators: planning, tool use, memory, reflection, multi-agent collaboration.

  • Reflexion / Self-Refine (Shinn et al.)
  • Multi-agent: AutoGen, CrewAI, LangGraph
  • Computer use (Anthropic), browser agents
  • Long-horizon tasks: Devin, OpenDevin, SWE-agent

World Models & JEPA

LeCun's vision: predictive world models (Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture) learning by observation, not just prediction.

  • Learn abstract representations, not pixels
  • Hierarchical planning (subgoals → actions)
  • Uncertainty estimation for safe exploration
  • I-JEPA, V-JEPA (Meta FAIR)

Neurosymbolic AI

Combine neural perception with symbolic reasoning: logic, programs, knowledge graphs.

  • Neural theorem provers (AlphaGeometry)
  • Program synthesis + LLMs (DreamCoder)
  • Knowledge graph retrieval + generation
  • Differentiable reasoning (DeepMind)

Multimodal Foundation Models

Native any-to-any: text, image, video, audio, 3D, sensor data in single model.

  • GPT-4o, Gemini 1.5, Claude 3.5 (early)
  • Video understanding: temporal reasoning
  • Robotics: π0, RT-2, OpenVLA
  • Scientific: multimodal molecules, climate

Efficient Architectures

Breaking quadratic attention: linear, sparse, hybrid architectures for infinite context.

  • Mamba / State Space Models (SSMs)
  • RWKV, Linear Attention, RetNet
  • Mixture of Experts (MoE): Mixtral, DeepSeekMoE
  • Quantization: 1.58-bit (BitNet), 4-bit norm

Scaling Laws & Compute Trends

The empirical rules driving AI progress—and where they might break.

The Scaling Hypothesis

Performance predictably improves with scale along three axes:

  • Parameters (N): Model size — more capacity for knowledge
  • Compute (C): Training FLOPs — more optimization steps
  • Data (D): Training tokens — more diverse examples

Chinchilla scaling (Hoffman et al. 2022): Optimal compute allocation → train ~20x more tokens per parameter than previously thought. Data matters as much as size.

Compute Trajectory

  • Training compute doubling ~6 months (2010-2024)
  • Cost: GPT-4 ~$100M; next gen $1B+ projected
  • Hardware: H100 → B200 → Rubin (2026) → next
  • Energy: Single training run = small city's annual use

Potential Bottlenecks

📊 Data Wall

High-quality text ~15T tokens. Exhausted ~2026. Synthetic data, video, code, multimodal as alternatives.

⚡ Energy & Chips

GW-scale data centers needed. Chip supply (TSMC CoWoS) constrained. Nuclear/solar buildout required.

🧠 Algorithmic Limits

Transformers may hit reasoning ceiling. New architectures needed for planning, world models.

💰 Economics

ROI must justify $100B+ investments. Revenue growth must match capex. Bubble risk if not.

Societal Transformation

How AI reshapes institutions, power, and daily life over the coming decades.

Science & Discovery

AI as scientific instrument: hypothesis generation, experiment design, literature synthesis.

  • Materials: millions of new stable crystals (GNoME)
  • Biology: protein design, drug discovery, cell simulation
  • Physics: fusion control, particle detector optimization
  • Mathematics: theorem proving (AlphaProof), conjecture generation

Power & Geopolitics

Compute as strategic resource. AI sovereignty, export controls, talent competition.

  • US-China: chip bans, talent restrictions, parallel ecosystems
  • EU: regulatory power (AI Act), digital sovereignty
  • Global South: leapfrog or dependency? Compute access inequality
  • Military: autonomous weapons, decision support, cyber

Healthcare Revolution

Personalized medicine, early detection, drug discovery acceleration.

  • Digital twins: simulate treatment before administering
  • Population screening: AI reads all scans, flags early
  • Drug design: months not years; rare diseases become viable
  • Global access: AI diagnostics on smartphones

Education & Work

Universal tutoring, credential transformation, continuous reskilling.

  • 1:1 AI tutor for every child (Khanmigo vision)
  • Skills-based hiring > degrees; portfolios + AI verification
  • Career paths: 5-10 job changes; AI career coaches
  • Creativity amplified: everyone a maker, not just consumers

Creative & Cultural

Democratized creation, new art forms, authenticity crisis.

  • Text-to-everything: movie, game, song, 3D world from prompt
  • Personalized media: stories/games generated for audience of one
  • Human-made premium: "certified human" as luxury signal
  • Copyright & ownership: unresolved legal framework

Governance & Institutions

AI in policy, law, administration. Speed vs. accountability.

  • Regulatory AI: simulate policy impacts before passing
  • Judicial: case prediction, bias detection, backlog reduction
  • Public services: personalized benefits, fraud detection
  • Democratic risk: microtargeting, deepfakes, epistemic fragmentation

How to Prepare

Robust strategies across uncertain futures. Focus on what's true in all scenarios.

Build AI Fluency Now

The single highest-ROI investment. Not coding—effective use.

  • Daily practice: 30 min with frontier models
  • Learn prompting deeply (it compounds)
  • Understand capabilities & limits concretely
  • Build a personal AI workflow

Develop Complementary Skills

What AI amplifies vs. replaces. Double down on the former.

  • Judgment & taste (knowing what's good)
  • Domain expertise + AI (the combination)
  • Human connection: leadership, care, negotiation
  • Systems thinking: orchestrate, don't just execute

Stay Adaptive

The half-life of skills is shrinking. Build learning muscle.

  • Follow 5-10 key researchers/labs on X/LinkedIn
  • Monthly: try one new tool, read one paper
  • Build in public: GitHub, blog, portfolio
  • Community: Discord, meetups, conferences

Financial & Career Resilience

Prepare for volatility. Options > predictions.

  • Emergency fund: 12+ months expenses
  • Diversified income: primary + side + investments
  • AI-exposed skills: audit your role's automatable %
  • Own AI equity: invest in infrastructure, not just apps
