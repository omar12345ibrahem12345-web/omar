The Simple Definition

Strip away the hype and complexity—here's what AI actually is.

AI = Computer Systems That Can Perform Tasks Requiring Human Intelligence

Instead of being explicitly programmed for every rule, AI systems learn patterns from data to make predictions, decisions, or generate content—much like how humans learn from experience.

What AI Is NOT

Clearing up common misconceptions helps you evaluate AI claims critically.

Not Human Intelligence

AI doesn't "think," "understand," or have consciousness. It processes patterns statistically—impressive mimicry, not genuine comprehension.

Understand the limits →

Not Infallible

AI makes mistakes, hallucinates facts, reflects training biases, and fails at reasoning tasks humans find trivial. It requires human oversight.

Learn about reliability →

Not Magic

Every AI output is the result of mathematical operations on training data. No reasoning, intent, or agency—just sophisticated pattern matching at scale.

Not a Single Thing

"AI" is an umbrella term covering many different technologies—machine learning, computer vision, NLP, robotics, expert systems, and more.
